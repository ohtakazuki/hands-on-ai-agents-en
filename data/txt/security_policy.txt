Aurora Works, Inc.
Information Security Policy

Section 1 — Purpose and Scope
1.1 Purpose
- Protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of Aurora Works information and systems.
- Provide clear, enforceable rules for data handling, device use, cloud services, incident reporting, and prohibited activities.

1.2 Scope
- Applies to all Aurora Works employees, contractors, temporary staff, consultants, vendors, and any third party that accesses Aurora Works information or systems (collectively “Users”).
- Covers all devices (company-owned and BYOD), networks, storage, applications, cloud services, and physical media that access or store Aurora Works data.

Section 2 — Roles and Responsibilities
2.1 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Final authority for security exceptions, incident escalation, risk acceptance, and third-party security approvals.

2.2 Information Security Team (InfoSec)
- Maintain policies, perform risk assessments, run vendor security reviews, manage the approved cloud services inventory, respond to incidents, monitor compliance.

2.3 IT Operations
- Enforce device enrollment, remote wipe, access provisioning and deprovisioning, patch management, and technical controls.

2.4 Managers
- Ensure team compliance, approve low-risk cloud trials, report incidents involving their staff within specified timeframes.

2.5 Users
- Follow this policy, complete required training, promptly report incidents, and protect credentials and company data.

Section 3 — Data Classification and Handling
3.1 Classification Levels
- Public: Information intended for public release. No restrictions.
- Internal: Non-sensitive business information intended for Aurora Works employees and contractors. Store on corporate drives; no special encryption required in transit if internal network used.
- Confidential: Business information that could harm Aurora Works or customers if disclosed (e.g., customer contact lists, internal product roadmaps). Must be encrypted at rest and in transit; access on least-privilege basis.
- Restricted: Highest sensitivity (e.g., personally identifiable information (PII), payment card data, legal privileged material, export-controlled data). Requires strong encryption at rest and in transit, strict access controls, logging, and retention/disposal per Legal/InfoSec direction.

3.2 Labeling and Storage
- All Confidential and Restricted information must be labeled in files, documents, and data stores using corporate metadata tags or file headers.
- Confidential/Restricted data must be stored only in approved corporate systems (see Section 7).

3.3 Transmission
- Confidential and Restricted data must be transmitted using company-approved, encrypted channels. Sending Confidential/Restricted data to personal email or personal cloud services is prohibited.

3.4 Retention and Disposal
- Follow retention schedules in Records Retention Policy. When disposal is required, use secure deletion or physical destruction methods approved by InfoSec. For Restricted data, certificate of destruction must be obtained and stored.

3.5 Reclassification and Exceptions
- Reclassification (up or down) must be approved by InfoSec in writing and documented in the Records Management system within 5 business days.

Section 4 — Passwords and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
4.1 Password Requirements
- Minimum length: 12 characters; passphrases encouraged.
- No password reuse of corporate passwords across Aurora Works accounts. Password manager required for storing work credentials.
- Passwords must not contain Aurora Works account names, employee names, or simple dictionary words only.

4.2 Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- MFA is mandatory for all Aurora Works accounts with access to Internal, Confidential, or Restricted data.
- MFA factors must be a combination of: something you know (password), something you have (authenticator app/hardware token), or something you are (biometrics). Authenticator app or hardware token preferred; SMS-based only allowed temporarily with manager and InfoSec approval.

4.3 Sharing and Delegation
- Sharing credentials is strictly prohibited. Shared account access must use the corporate password manager or role-based accounts provisioned by IT with logged activity.

4.4 Password Changes
- Forced password rotation every 180 days is required only for privileged accounts. Otherwise, change passwords immediately if compromise is suspected or as requested by IT/InfoSec.

4.5 Exceptions
- Exceptions to MFA or password rules require an exception request submitted to InfoSec and approval by the CISO for any production or Restricted-system exception. Temporary exceptions may be granted for up to 30 days; extensions require CISO approval.

Section 5 — Device Management
5.1 Company-Issued Devices
- Must be enrolled in Aurora Works Mobile Device Management (MDM), full-disk encrypted, run company-approved AV/EDR, have screen-lock enabled after 5 minutes, and receive OS/security updates within 14 calendar days of release.
- Installation of unauthorized software is prohibited.

5.2 Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
- BYOD is permitted only if:
  - Device is enrolled and managed through MDM.
  - A corporate container is used for company data separation.
  - Device is not jailbroken or rooted.
  - Only Internal and, with special approval, Confidential data may be accessed from BYOD. Restricted data is prohibited on BYOD.
- If BYOD enrollment is refused, User will be denied access to corporate systems.

5.3 Lost or Stolen Devices
- Report within 4 hours to InfoSec (security@auroraworks.inc) and to IT (internal extension 5555). IT may perform remote wipe immediately after report or after CISO authorization for devices with Restricted data.
- Failure to report within 24 hours without reasonable explanation is a policy violation.

5.4 Prohibited Device Conduct
- Rooting, jailbreaking, disabling security controls, or removing MDM is prohibited. Any device found non-compliant will be blocked from corporate access.

Section 6 — Removal of Company Assets and Data (Including Offboarding)
6.1 General Rule
- Company data and assets may not be removed from company control except via approved methods and systems.

6.2 Personal Devices and External Media
- Storing Confidential or Restricted data on personal devices, personal cloud accounts, or removable media (USB drives, SD cards) is prohibited unless explicitly authorized by InfoSec and the device is encrypted and managed.

6.3 Offboarding Procedure
- HR must notify IT and InfoSec no later than one (1) business day prior to employee departure or immediately upon termination.
- IT will disable system access within 24 hours of last working day/time. For involuntary terminations, IT will disable access immediately upon HR notification.
- Company-owned devices must be returned within five (5) business days. IT will inventory returned devices, perform secure wipe, and certify completion.
- Manager and IT must confirm inventory and deletion via an offboarding checklist and store documentation in HR records.

6.4 Legal Holds
- If Legal places a record or device under legal hold, the device/data must not be wiped. Legal and InfoSec will document hold and instruct IT within 24 hours.

Section 7 — Approved Cloud Services and Vendor Use
7.1 Approved Services Inventory
- Aurora Works maintains an Approved Cloud Services Inventory (ACSI) managed by InfoSec and IT. Only services on the ACSI may be used for storing or processing Aurora Works Confidential or Restricted data.

7.2 Approval Process for New Cloud Services
- To add a service, submit vendor security package to InfoSec including:
  - Vendor security questionnaire or SOC2-equivalent report.
  - Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and standard contractual clauses if applicable.
  - Data residency and encryption details.
  - Penetration test or security assessment summary (if available).
  - Business justification and data types to be stored.
- InfoSec will complete review within ten (10) business days. Legal review occur in parallel; final approval by InfoSec and Legal is required before production use.

7.3 Trial and Temporary Use
- Managers may request trial access for evaluation for up to thirty (30) calendar days. Trial must be registered with InfoSec and must not be used for Confidential or Restricted data unless explicitly approved.

7.4 Prohibitions
- Use of personal cloud services (personal email providers, personal file-sharing) for company Confidential or Restricted data is prohibited.

Section 8 — Incident Reporting and Response
8.1 What Constitutes an Incident
- Unauthorized access, data loss/theft, data exposure, malware/ransomware, suspicious network traffic, lost/stolen devices with company data, phishing compromising credentials, or any activity that could impact confidentiality, integrity, or availability.

8.2 Reporting Timelines and Channels
- Critical incidents (active data breach, ransomware, unauthorized access to Restricted data): Report immediately, and no later than two (2) hours, to InfoSec via security@auroraworks.inc and Security On-call (ext. 5555).
- Non-critical incidents: Report within twenty-four (24) hours via the corporate incident reporting portal or security@auroraworks.inc.
- All Users must create an incident ticket; managers must confirm reporting for their teams within one (1) hour of learning of an incident.

8.3 Immediate Actions by Reporter
- If possible, isolate affected device(s) from network but do not power down unless instructed.
- Preserve evidence: do not alter logs, delete files, or clear browser history.
- Provide as much detail as available: who, what, when, where, suspected cause.

8.4 Incident Handling Workflow
- InfoSec triage begins within one (1) hour of notification.
- Severity assessment and containment actions executed within four (4) hours for critical incidents.
- Escalation to CISO, Legal, and Communications occurs for incidents classified as severe (data breach affecting Restricted data, regulatory impact).
- Customer and regulator notification responsibilities determined within seventy-two (72) hours in coordination with Legal; timelines follow applicable laws.

8.5 Post-Incident Requirements
- InfoSec will produce an incident report with timeline, root cause, impact assessment, remediation plan, and lessons learned within ten (10) business days.
- Affected systems shall remain offline or in controlled state until InfoSec authorizes return to production.

Section 9 — Prohibited Activities (Explicit)
9.1 Prohibited without Exception
- Sharing Aurora Works credentials with others, or storing them in an unsecured location.
- Installing, running, or distributing malware, ransomware, or hacking tools on company systems.
- Deliberately scanning or probing internal or external Aurora Works systems without prior written approval from InfoSec.
- Bypassing or disabling security controls (firewalls, MDM, EDR, MFA).
- Storing Confidential or Restricted data on personal cloud storage, personal email accounts, or on non-approved SaaS.
- Connecting non-managed devices to production networks or critical infrastructure.
- Removing company data or devices during employment termination if under legal hold.
- Exporting data subject to export controls without Legal and InfoSec approval.
- Engaging in social engineering attacks or test intrusion without documented InfoSec authorization.
- Unauthorized use of anonymizers/proxies to hide activity on Aurora Works systems.

9.2 Consequences
- Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, civil action, and referral to law enforcement. Access will be suspended immediately upon credible evidence of violation.

Section 10 — Exceptions and Approval Workflow
10.1 Standard Exception Request
- Submit exception request through the SecOps portal with:
  - Description of exception and systems affected.
  - Business justification and requested duration (maximum 90 days).
  - Risk mitigation and compensating controls.
- InfoSec will review and respond within five (5) business days. CISO approval required for high-risk or >30-day exceptions.
- All granted exceptions are logged and reviewed quarterly. Expired exceptions automatically revoke.

10.2 Emergency Exceptions
- CISO or IT Director may grant verbal emergency exceptions to restore business operations. Verbal approvals must be followed by a written exception request and full documentation within 24 hours. Emergency exceptions expire after seven (7) days unless extended in writing.

Section 11 — Compliance, Auditing, and Enforcement
11.1 Monitoring and Audits
- InfoSec will perform continuous monitoring, quarterly audits of access and the ACSI, and annual penetration tests. Users must cooperate with audits.

11.2 Employee Attestations and Training
- All Users must complete security awareness training within thirty (30) days of hire and annual refreshers. Managers must enforce completion—failure to complete within 14 days of deadline may result in access restrictions.

11.3 Enforcement
- Non-compliance may result in access revocation, disciplinary action per HR policies, and legal actions where appropriate. Significant violations trigger an incident investigation by InfoSec and HR/Legal.

11.4 Whistleblower Protections
- Users reporting incidents or violations in good faith will not be retaliated against. Reports may be anonymous where supported by reporting channels.

Section 12 — Definitions
- Access Provisioning: Process to grant accounts/permissions.
- ACSI: Approved Cloud Services Inventory maintained by InfoSec.
- Confidential Data: See Section 3.1.
- MDM: Mobile Device Management.
- MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication.
- Restricted Data: See Section 3.1.
- SecOps Portal: Aurora Works internal security request and incident system.
