Aurora Works, Inc.
Employee Handbook (Excerpt)

Section 1 — Scope and Purpose
1.1 Purpose: This excerpt establishes clear, actionable rules for work hours, scheduling, remote work, leave/time off, tardiness/early departures, outside employment, performance reviews, disciplinary actions, and HR reporting channels.
1.2 Applicability: Applies to all Aurora Works employees (full‑time, part‑time, exempt, non‑exempt) based in the U.S. where local law allows. Where state or federal law imposes greater rights, that law controls.

Section 2 — Work Hours, Flexible Scheduling, and Core Hours
2.1 Standard Work Week
- Exempt employees: standard expectation is 40 hours per week; hours scheduled by manager to meet business needs.
- Non‑exempt employees: scheduled hours must be recorded and approved; overtime must be pre‑approved by the manager.

2.2 Core Hours and Coverage
- Core hours are 10:00 AM – 3:00 PM local time, Monday–Friday. Employees must be available for meetings and immediate team collaboration during core hours unless an approved exception exists.
- Teams that must support cross‑country customers may establish team‑specific core hours with manager and HR approval.

2.3 Flexible Scheduling
- Aurora Works supports flexible start/stop times outside core hours to accommodate personal needs and time zones.
- Approval workflow:
  1. Employee submits a Flexible Schedule Request in the HR portal at least 10 business days before start.
  2. Manager reviews availability impact and either approves, proposes modifications, or denies within 5 business days.
  3. HR reviews for policy compliance and finalizes within 3 business days.
- Exceptions: Flexible schedules may be disallowed for roles requiring fixed coverage (e.g., scheduled customer support) or where performance/customer service would be impacted. Manager must document rationale when denying.

Section 3 — Remote and Hybrid Work
3.1 General Policy
- Aurora Works is hybrid/remote‑friendly. Many roles are eligible for full or partial remote work; others require regular on‑site presence. Eligibility is role‑based and determined by manager and HR.
3.2 Permanent Remote Work Outside Commutable Distance
- Employees wishing to work permanently outside the commutable area must submit a Remote Work Location Request at least 30 days before relocation.
- Approval requires manager sign‑off and HR review for tax, payroll, and benefits impacts; final approval may take up to 15 business days.
3.3 Equipment and Security
- Company equipment must be used for Aurora work only. Remote employees must follow the IT security policy (passwords, VPN, data encryption). Personal devices require advance IT approval.
3.4 Workspace Safety
- Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe ergonomic home workspace. The company may provide a one‑time stipend per remote employee as per the IT/Facilities policy (separate document).
3.5 On‑Site Expectations
- If a role requires periodic on‑site work, managers will provide schedule at least 2 weeks in advance.

Section 4 — Time Off and Leave
4.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)
- Accrual schedule:
  - 0–2 years service: 15 days (120 hours) per year.
  - 3–6 years: 20 days (160 hours) per year.
  - 7+ years: 25 days (200 hours) per year.
- PTO accrues per pay period, available after 90 days of employment unless local law requires immediate availability.
- Carryover: Up to 40 hours PTO may be carried over to the next calendar year; excess hours are forfeited unless otherwise required by law.

4.2 Requesting PTO
- Single‑day requests: submit via HR portal at least 48 hours in advance.
- Multi‑day requests (3+ consecutive days): submit at least 14 calendar days in advance.
- Manager response deadline: 5 business days. If manager does not respond, HR will follow up and may auto‑approve for non‑critical coverage needs.

4.3 Sick Leave and Short‑term Illness
- Sick time is included in PTO. For absences of three or more consecutive days due to illness, employees must notify their manager and may be asked to provide medical documentation.

4.4 Family Leave and FMLA
- Eligible employees may take unpaid leave under FMLA (12 weeks in a 12‑month period) subject to federal eligibility (12 months of employment and 1,250 hours worked in the prior 12 months).
- To request FMLA: submit a Leave Request to HR at least 30 days in advance when practicable. HR will notify employees of eligibility and required medical certification within 5 business days.
- Required documentation: medical certification must be provided within 15 calendar days of HR’s request, unless impracticable.

4.5 Parental Leave
- Aurora Works provides 6 weeks paid primary caregiver leave (in addition to any applicable PTO) for birth or adoption, subject to eligibility and documentation.
- Notice: Employees should provide at least 30 days’ notice where practicable; otherwise as soon as possible.

4.6 Bereavement and Jury Duty
- Bereavement: Up to 5 paid days for immediate family; up to 2 paid days for extended family. Manager and HR must be notified as soon as practicable. Documentation (e.g., obituary) may be requested.
- Jury Duty: Paid for required jury service. Employee must submit the jury summons to HR within 3 business days of receipt.

4.7 Unpaid Leave and Special Circumstances
- Unpaid leave may be granted on a case‑by‑case basis with manager and HR approval. Request at least 30 days in advance when possible. Approval requires a documented plan for benefits, return date, and coverage.

Section 5 — Tardiness and Early Departures
5.1 Expectations and Notification
- Employees must record work hours in the company timekeeping system. For tardiness or early departures, employees must notify their manager as soon as possible and record the time entry.
5.2 Documentation and Thresholds
- Emergency/one‑time occurrences: excused when promptly reported.
- Repeated occurrences:
  - 3 occurrences within a 90‑day period: manager will issue a verbal counseling and document in the employee file.
  - 5 occurrences within a 90‑day period: written warning.
  - Continued occurrences after warnings: subject to PIP or further discipline up to termination.
- Medical exceptions: If tardiness/early departures are due to a documented medical condition, provide medical certification; HR and manager will engage in an accommodation discussion under applicable law.

5.3 Timekeeping Falsification
- Falsifying time records, approving inaccurate timesheets, or otherwise manipulating timekeeping is prohibited and subject to immediate disciplinary action, up to termination.

Section 6 — Outside Employment (Moonlighting)
6.1 Permitted and Prohibited Activities
- Outside employment is permitted if:
  - It does not create a conflict of interest.
  - It does not interfere with Aurora Works’ duties, scheduled hours, or performance.
  - It does not use Aurora Works’ confidential information or resources.
- Prohibited:
  - Any work for a direct competitor, vendor under contract with Aurora Works where conflict exists, or in any role that compromises company IP or customer relationships.
  - Using company time, systems, or resources for outside work.
6.2 Disclosure and Approval Process
- Employees must submit an Outside Employment Disclosure Form to HR at least 10 business days prior to starting outside work.
- Manager and HR will review for conflicts within 7 business days and provide written approval, conditional approval, or denial. If denied, the employee must cease the outside employment or submit a mitigation plan.
6.3 Enforcement
- Failure to disclose or follow restrictions is grounds for disciplinary action, up to termination.

Section 7 — Performance Reviews and Improvement
7.1 Review Cycle
- Annual performance review for all employees. Mid‑year check‑in required for all direct reports.
7.2 Process and Documentation
- Timeline:
  1. Self‑assessment submitted in HR portal 7 calendar days before the review meeting.
  2. Manager assessment completed and meeting scheduled; manager provides written goals and rating discussion.
  3. Final review document signed by employee and manager within 5 business days after the meeting.
- Ratings scale: Exceeds Expectations / Meets Expectations / Needs Improvement / Unsatisfactory (organization may use a numeric equivalent).
7.3 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
- When performance is “Needs Improvement” or “Unsatisfactory,” a PIP may be issued with:
  - Specific, measurable objectives.
  - Resources/training to be provided.
  - Duration (typically 30–90 calendar days).
  - Scheduled check‑ins (minimum weekly for non‑exempt, bi‑weekly for exempt roles) and documentation requirements.
- Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in demotion, reassignment, suspension, or termination.

Section 8 — Disciplinary Actions
8.1 Progressive Discipline Framework
- Typical progression (may vary based on severity):
  1. Verbal counseling (documented).
  2. Written warning.
  3. Final written warning or suspension.
  4. Termination.
- Immediate termination may occur for severe misconduct including but not limited to: theft, fraud, harassment, violence, egregious confidentiality breaches, or timekeeping falsification.

8.2 Documentation and Appeals
- All disciplinary actions will be documented in the HR file. Employees may submit a written response to a disciplinary action within 10 business days; the response will be retained with the disciplinary record.

Section 9 — Reporting Channels, Confidentiality, and Non‑Retaliation
9.1 How to Report
- HR email (internal): hr@auroraworks.internal
- HR portal: Submit confidential case via the HR portal (link available on company intranet).
- Anonymous reporting hotline: 1‑800‑000‑0000 (third‑party service) or via company hotline web form.
9.2 What to Report
- Workplace harassment, discrimination, safety incidents, ethics concerns, payroll errors, policy violations, retaliation concerns, or other compliance issues.
9.3 Response Time and Process
- HR acknowledges receipt within 3 business days.
- Initial assessment within 5 business days; investigative timeframe depends on complexity but HR will provide status updates at least every 10 business days.
9.4 Confidentiality and Protections
- Investigations are confidential to the extent feasible. Aurora Works prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith or participates in an investigation. Retaliation should be reported immediately; violations result in disciplinary action up to termination.

Section 10 — Enforcement and Exceptions
10.1 Policy Enforcement
- Managers are responsible for consistent application of these policies. HR is responsible for oversight, recordkeeping, and legal compliance checks.
10.2 Exceptions
- Any exception to this excerpt must be:
  1. Requested in writing.
  2. Reviewed and approved by the employee’s manager and HR.
  3. Documented in writing with a clear end date (exceptions are typically temporary and will not create precedent).
- Emergency exceptions may be granted by the VP of People or designee; HR will document rationale and duration.
