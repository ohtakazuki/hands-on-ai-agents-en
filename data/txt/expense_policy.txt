Aurora Works, Inc.
Expense Reimbursement Policy

Section 1 — Purpose and Scope
1.1 Purpose: Establish clear rules for reimbursement of reasonable, business-related expenses incurred by Aurora Works employees while performing authorized work.
1.2 Scope: Applies to all Aurora Works employees, contractors when explicitly authorized in writing, and any third party submitting expenses on behalf of employees. Primarily U.S.-based policy — where local law differs, employees must follow local law plus Finance guidance.

Section 2 — General Principles
2.1 Expenses must be: authorized in advance when required, reasonable in amount, supported by required documentation, and directly related to Aurora Works business.
2.2 Preferred payment methods: company corporate card (if issued) or employee personal card with subsequent reimbursement.
2.3 Duplicate reimbursement (double-dipping) is prohibited.

Section 3 — Definitions
3.1 Business Travel: Overnight trips or travel requiring more than two hours away from an employee’s primary work location for company business.
3.2 Local Transportation: Short trips within an employee’s normal work area (e.g., rideshares, taxis, parking, public transit, mileage for personal vehicle use).
3.3 Client Entertainment: Meals or events paid for to influence, retain, or obtain business where at least one non-Aurora Works third-party guest is present.

Section 4 — Pre-Approval and Authorization
4.1 Travel Authorization: All business travel must be approved in the expense system prior to booking. Authorization request must include: business purpose, estimated total cost, dates, attendees, and preferred bookings.
4.2 Approval thresholds:
  - Manager approval required for routine travel and expenses up to $2,500.
  - Director/Finance Manager approval required for estimated trip costs between $2,501 and $7,500.
  - VP approval required for estimated trip costs over $7,500.
  - Any exception to policy (lodging over caps, premium cabin travel, extended per diems) requires written pre-approval by the Finance Director or CFO.
4.3 Timeframe: Submit requests for expected approvals at least 10 business days before travel. For urgent travel, obtain manager pre-approval and document reasons; retroactive Finance approval must be requested within 3 business days after return.

Section 5 — Local Transportation
5.1 Rideshare/Taxi/Public Transit: Reimbursable for business trips with itemized receipt. Provide business purpose on expense report.
5.2 Personal Vehicle Mileage:
  - Reimbursed at the current federal standard mileage rate in effect on the date of travel (employees must confirm the current rate with Finance when submitting).
  - A mileage log with start/end odometer or mapping link and business purpose required.
5.3 Parking and Tolls: Reimbursable with receipt. Long-term parking for travel requires pre-approval if cost exceeds $50/day.
5.4 Personal Use: Personal detours or commuting between home and primary worksite are not reimbursable.

Section 6 — Air Travel
6.1 Booking: All air travel should be booked through Aurora Works’ designated travel tool or travel vendor unless expressly exempted by Finance.
6.2 Cabin Class:
  - Domestic flights under six (6) hours: economy class only.
  - Domestic flights six (6) hours or longer: premium economy allowed with manager approval; requires Director/Finance approval if cost delta exceeds $500.
  - International flights: economy or premium economy; business class requires VP approval.
6.3 Upgrades: Upgrades (including seat selection fees, extra legroom) are not reimbursable unless pre-approved.
6.4 Changes/Cancellations: Employees must use lowest reasonably available fare for changed travel; penalties for voluntary changes are the employee’s responsibility unless approved for business reason.

Section 7 — Lodging
7.1 Booking: Use company travel tool/hotel program where available. Preferred hotels and negotiated rates should be used when practical.
7.2 Reimbursement Caps:
  - Standard domestic lodging cap: $250 per night (excluding taxes and mandatory fees).
  - High-cost market: Finance publishes a high-cost city list where an elevated cap (up to $400/night) may apply; elevated rates require manager plus Finance approval.
  - International lodging caps to be determined on a per-trip basis by Finance.
7.3 Receipts: Itemized hotel folio required showing room rate, taxes, and itemized incidental charges. Personal incidentals (e.g., mini-bar, hotel movies, spa) are non-reimbursable.
7.4 Room Sharing: Allowed when documented and pre-approved. Reimbursement limited to the employee’s share.

Section 8 — Meals and Per Diem
8.1 Two reimbursement options (choose one per trip):
  - Actual expense: Reimbursed up to $75/day (U.S. domestic standard), supported by itemized receipts including tax and tip.
  - Per diem: Flat $60/day for meals & incidental expenses (M&IE). First and last travel day at 75% of per diem. If Aurora Works provides a meal (conference, catered meeting), per diem is reduced for that meal day proportionally.
8.2 Alcohol:
  - Personal travel meals: alcohol is not reimbursable.
  - Client entertainment meals: limited rules in Section 9.
8.3 Tips: Gratuities up to 25% are reimbursable as part of meal receipts.

Section 9 — Client Meals and Entertainment
9.1 Eligibility: Reimbursable when client entertainment has clear business purpose and is likely to produce a direct business benefit.
9.2 Documentation: For every client meal/entertainment expense provide: date, location, business purpose, names and titles/organizations of attendees (Aurora Works and external), and itemized receipt.
9.3 Per-person limits:
  - Standard client meal limit: $150 per person (including tax and tip).
  - Alcohol at client meals: reimbursable up to $30 per person with manager approval and documentation.
  - Any single event exceeding $500 total requires Director approval; events exceeding $2,000 require VP approval and pre-authorization.
9.4 Prohibitions: Reimbursement for entertainment of a family member or friend who is not part of the business activity is not allowed unless pre-approved by a VP and recorded with business justification.

Section 10 — Receipts and Documentation
10.1 Required Receipts: Itemized receipts required for all reimbursable expenses except:
  - Per diem claims.
  - Local transit fares under $25 (employees are encouraged to capture receipts when available).
10.2 Digital Receipts: Acceptable if legible and tamper-evident. Upload to expense system linked to the corresponding expense line.
10.3 Insufficient Documentation: If a receipt is missing, employee must obtain manager approval and written explanation; Finance may deny reimbursement if explanation is insufficient.
10.4 Retention: Employees must retain original documentation for at least two (2) years; Finance will retain records per corporate retention schedule.

Section 11 — Submission Deadlines and Reimbursement Timing
11.1 Submission Deadlines:
  - Submit expense reports within 30 calendar days of the expense date or return from business travel, whichever is later.
  - Expenses submitted between 31–60 days require manager acknowledgment and rationale. Finance may process but reserves right to deny.
  - Expenses submitted after 60 days will be denied except with documented, pre-approved exceptions signed by the Finance Director.
11.2 Reimbursement Timing: Once approved, Finance will reimburse employees within ten (10) business days. Reimbursements may be issued via payroll deduction if employee owes the company funds.

Section 12 — Exceptions and Approval Workflow
12.1 Standard Exceptions:
  - Emergency or last-minute travel: Manager pre-approval required; request retroactive Finance approval within 3 business days.
  - Lodging over cap or premium cabin travel: Written pre-approval by Finance Director (or CFO for senior leadership).
12.2 Approval Documentation: All exceptions must be documented in the expense system with explicit approver name, date, and business justification.
12.3 Emergency Exceptions: Finance reserves right to require additional documentation or impose repayment if exception lacks sufficient justification.

Section 13 — Prohibited Actions
13.1 The following are strictly prohibited:
  - Falsifying, altering, or fabricating receipts or expense documentation.
  - Splitting charges to circumvent approval thresholds or caps.
  - Charging personal expenses (household, non-business travel, leisure) to Aurora Works.
  - Booking travel through alternate persons to avoid travel policy or company rates.
  - Submitting duplicate expenses for the same item.
13.2 Consequences for prohibited actions are described in Section 14.

Section 14 — Enforcement and Consequences for Violations
14.1 Administrative Remedies:
  - Denial of reimbursement for non-compliant claims.
  - Requirement to repay Aurora Works for disallowed amounts; repayment due within 30 days or via payroll deduction with employee consent where legally permitted.
14.2 Disciplinary Actions:
  - First documented non-fraud violation: written counseling and mandatory policy retraining.
  - Repeated or material violations: formal written warning, suspension of travel privileges, or other corrective action up to termination.
14.3 Fraud or Intentional Misconduct:
  - Intentional falsification or theft will result in immediate termination and may be referred for civil or criminal prosecution.
14.4 Recovery of Funds: Aurora Works may offset future reimbursements or wages to recover disallowed expenses to the extent permitted by law.

Section 15 — Audits and Compliance
15.1 Periodic audits of expense reports will be performed by Finance and Internal Audit. Employees must cooperate and provide requested documentation within five (5) business days.
15.2 Noncompliant patterns may trigger broader reviews and corrective action.

Section 16 — Related Policies and Contacts
16.1 Related policies: Travel Safety & Risk Policy, Corporate Card Policy, Code of Conduct.
16.2 Questions and Approvals: Contact Finance (finance-ops@auroraworks.inc) for policy clarifications, approval routing, and high-cost market lists. Managers must route exception approvals through the expense system to be valid.

Section 17 — Miscellaneous
17.1 Policy Changes: Aurora Works may amend this policy at any time. Employees will be notified of material changes.
17.2 Local Law: Where local law provides greater employee protections or different requirements, local law controls.
